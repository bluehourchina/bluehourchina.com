With 15 days, I would choose the fourth stop by friction, not by fame.

You already have Shanghai, Xi'an and Beijing, so the extra nature stop should not create too many flights, transfers or hotel changes.

My practical ranking would be:

1. Huangshan + Hongcun: lowest-friction nature/culture add-on from Shanghai by train. Good if budget and efficiency matter.
2. Jiuzhaigou + Huanglong: probably the strongest pure scenery choice, but it adds more moving parts around Chengdu and the park transfers.
3. Guilin/Yangshuo: iconic karst scenery and easier than many western-China routes, though it can feel more touristy.
4. Yunnan: beautiful and a very different China, but Dali/Shaxi/Lijiang really wants 5-6 days to feel worthwhile. I would only choose it if you are willing to reduce time elsewhere.

For your dates, I would probably choose Huangshan if you want the cleanest classic-route add-on, Jiuzhaigou if the priority is dramatic scenery, and Yunnan only if the priority is slower atmosphere rather than simply adding one scenic stop.

If you want, reply with how many nights you planned for Shanghai, Xi'an and Beijing, plus your rough budget/comfort level, and I can suggest a cleaner 15-day skeleton.

Small 2026 update / framing point: I would still plan China payment as layers, not as one perfect method.

Set up Alipay and/or WeChat Pay before departure where available, keep a second card, carry a small RMB cash layer, and make sure the phone number tied to the cards can receive SMS verification. The first few payment failures are often issuer/SMS/setup problems rather than a lack of merchants.

For travellers going beyond Beijing and Shanghai, payment setup becomes part of route comfort. If you are moving through Yunnan, Xinjiang, Dunhuang or smaller towns, the stressful moments are often not the scenery but taxi pickup, hotel communication, ticketing, late arrivals and whether one app suddenly refuses a payment.

I would also not plan around any brand-new payment feature until it has been tested on the ground. Useful news can help, but a backup card + cash + working SMS is still the safer travel plan.

For F63 and M72, I would choose the route by comfort and risk, not by the most dramatic view.

The key questions are: knee condition, fear of heights, tolerance for crowds, and whether a long walk after the cable car would make the rest of the day unpleasant.

If both travellers are steady walkers and not afraid of heights, the glass-platform route can be memorable. If either person is cautious with knees, balance or vertigo, I would keep the route simpler: use the cable car/escalator-heavy option, reduce unnecessary walking, and leave enough margin for queues.

For senior travellers, Zhangjiajie is one of those places where the "best" route is often the one that prevents exhaustion. I would not combine too many scenic zones in one day. Pick one main mountain experience, start early, and keep the evening light.

If you share the travel month, hotel area, whether they can manage stairs, and whether heights are a concern, I can narrow this to the safer route.

For Guangzhou with a 5-year-old, I would choose by neighbourhood and daily rhythm before choosing by brand.

The luxury hotels in Guangzhou can be very good value compared with Europe, but the experience feels different depending on area.

Four Seasons / Ritz style choices are usually stronger if you want a polished, high-rise, business-district stay around Tianhe and do not mind using taxis for most things.

White Swan is worth considering if you want a more classic Guangzhou feeling, river/Shamian atmosphere and a softer family pace. It can feel less "international tower" and more rooted in the city.

Voco can make sense if you prefer value and local street access, but I would not choose only by price with a 5-year-old. Lobby comfort, breakfast, taxi access and whether the surrounding area is easy after dinner matter more than they look on a booking page.

For mid-November, I would first decide whether you want Guangzhou to feel like a polished city stop or a gentler family base. Then the hotel choice becomes clearer.
