02 · No-link answer

支付問題先手回覆

For China, I would plan payment in layers rather than relying on one method.

Set up WeChat Pay and/or Alipay before departure where available, keep a second overseas card, carry a small RMB cash layer, and make sure the phone number tied to your cards can receive SMS verification. Many first payment failures are issuer, SMS or setup problems, not merchant problems.

This matters more once you go beyond Beijing and Shanghai. In Yunnan, Xinjiang, Dunhuang, Zhangjiajie or smaller towns, payment readiness affects taxi pickup, ticketing, hotel communication and transfer days.

Which month are you going, and are you mostly staying in big cities or going into smaller scenic regions?

03 · App / SMS answer

App 與簡訊問題先手回覆

Before China, I would check four practical things:

1. Mobile payment setup and a backup card/cash layer
2. A phone plan that can receive SMS verification
3. Map, translation and ride-hailing backup
4. Hotel names and addresses saved in Chinese

The destination is only half the trip. The other half is whether the day can move smoothly when you are tired, arriving late or outside the biggest cities.

Are you planning a first China trip, or are you going beyond Beijing/Shanghai this time?

04 · PayPal / WeChat update

2026 支付新聞角度

Useful 2026 update: PayPal / WeChat Pay news is a good signal that China is trying to reduce payment friction for foreign visitors.

But I would still not plan a trip around one new feature. The safer setup is layered: WeChat Pay or Alipay where available, an overseas card backup, small RMB cash, and a phone number that can receive SMS.

If your route goes into Yunnan, Xinjiang, Dunhuang, Zhangjiajie or other scenic regions, payment setup becomes part of route comfort, not just an app question.

06 · After they reply

對方回覆後，只問 5 個條件

Great. Before suggesting any route, I need five practical details:

1. Travel month or rough season
2. Group size and whether there are seniors or children
3. Number of days outside Beijing/Shanghai
4. Places you are considering, or the feeling you want
5. Email / WhatsApp / WeChat / LINE for follow-up

With those details, we can reply with a first route note: whether the route is realistic, what the pacing should feel like, and the starting quote direction.
