If your card, Alipay or WeChat Pay stops working in China, do not make tomorrow's route tighter.

Make the route calmer first.

Separate the problem:
- card issuer
- app verification
- merchant scenario

自然景點 15 天決策

approved manualfit 10route decision
For a first China trip with 15 days, I would not add a natural wonder only by fame. I would choose by transfer friction and recovery time.

If Shanghai and Beijing are already fixed, Zhangjiajie is the most dramatic choice but needs careful pacing. Guilin/Yangshuo is softer and easier to combine. Yunnan is richer emotionally, but it works best when you give it enough days instead of treating it as a quick add-on.

The real question is: after Shanghai and before Beijing, do you want one dramatic landscape, or a slower regional rhythm with old towns and mountain distance?

How many nights can you protect for the nature part, excluding arrival and departure days?

Money matters / payment in China

approved manualfit 9payment
For foreign visitors, I would treat China payment as a layered setup, not a single app setup.

Have at least three layers:
1. Alipay or WeChat Pay with your foreign card linked and tested
2. A backup card from a different issuer if possible
3. A small RMB cash layer for moments when apps, SMS or merchant acceptance create friction

The practical risk is not only whether a payment works once. It is whether it works when you are tired, carrying luggage, checking into a hotel, taking a taxi, buying tickets or transferring in a smaller city.

If your trip goes beyond Beijing and Shanghai, payment readiness becomes part of route comfort.

張家界長者路線

approved manualfit 10senior comfort
For two senior travellers, I would choose Zhangjiajie by comfort and risk before views.

The key checks are knees, balance, fear of heights, queue tolerance and how much walking is still comfortable after a cable car or escalator. If either traveller is cautious with stairs or vertigo, keep the day simpler and avoid combining too many scenic zones.

For Tianmen Mountain, a route with cable car / shuttle / escalator support is usually more comfortable than chasing every viewpoint. It is better to finish one area calmly than to make the next day suffer.

What month are they travelling, and are stairs or heights the bigger concern?

廣州高端酒店家庭

approved manualfit 9family hotel
With a 5-year-old, I would choose Guangzhou hotel area by daily rhythm before brand.

Tianhe luxury hotels can be excellent for polished service, views and business-district convenience. White Swan / Shamian can feel gentler, more walkable and more rooted in the city. A value hotel can work only if breakfast, taxi pickup, lobby comfort and evening surroundings still feel easy with a child.

If this is a short Guangzhou stop, convenience and sleep quality matter more than squeezing in more sights. If it connects to a wider China route, the next transfer should guide the hotel choice.

Is Guangzhou a short city stop, or are you connecting onward by train or airport?
