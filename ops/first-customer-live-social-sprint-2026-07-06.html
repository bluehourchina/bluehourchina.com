01 · Payment

WeChat Pay / Alipay / Visa

I would plan China payments in layers rather than relying on one perfect method.

Set up WeChat Pay and/or Alipay before departure where available, keep a second overseas card, carry a small RMB cash layer, and make sure the phone number tied to your cards can receive SMS verification. Many first payment failures are issuer, SMS or setup problems, not merchant problems.

This matters more once you go beyond Beijing and Shanghai. In Yunnan, Xinjiang, Dunhuang, Zhangjiajie or smaller towns, payment readiness affects taxi pickup, ticketing, hotel communication and transfer days.

Which month are you going, and are you staying mostly in big cities or going into smaller scenic regions?

02 · Yunnan

雲南路線太趕

For Yunnan, I would decide the rhythm before adding more places.

Dali, Shaxi and Lijiang/Baisha are not just dots on a map. Each needs a different kind of time. The common mistake is changing cities every day and then losing the quiet part of the trip.

If you have about a week, I would usually protect one slower Dali day, one Shaxi night, and then use Lijiang or Baisha for the snow-mountain side. The right shape depends less on famous sights and more on hotel location, car time, luggage and how tired the group gets.

What month are you going, how many travellers, and how many full days do you have in Yunnan?

03 · Beyond mega cities

二次來中國

If you have already done Beijing or Shanghai, I would choose the next China route by friction level, not only by beauty.

Yunnan is good when you want old towns, snow-mountain distance and a slower atmosphere. Zhangjiajie is dramatic but needs careful pacing. Dunhuang is beautiful if you want desert and Silk Road feeling. Xinjiang can be extraordinary, but it is large and needs more route control.

For a second China trip, the better question is: what can feel comfortable with your dates, transfer tolerance and hotel expectations?

How many days do you have outside the big cities?

04 · Xinjiang / Dunhuang

大尺度路線

For Xinjiang or Dunhuang, I would be careful with map distance. Western China can look simple on a map but feel very large on the ground.

The route should protect arrival time, driver days, hotel quality, language handoff and weather season. The beautiful parts are often not the problem; the problem is too many long transfers stacked together.

If your group wants comfort, I would rather reduce one stop and keep the route calm than collect every famous name.

Which month are you travelling, and are you more interested in desert, lakes, grassland or Silk Road history?

05 · Senior comfort

長者 / 親子舒適度

For senior travellers or families, I would choose the route by comfort first.

The route is not only attractions. It is breakfast rhythm, taxi pickup, walking distance, stairs, luggage, hotel location, ticket timing, language support and whether the evening still feels calm.

The best-looking route on paper can be a bad route if every day asks too much from the group.

Are there seniors, children, knee issues or a strong preference for slower mornings?
