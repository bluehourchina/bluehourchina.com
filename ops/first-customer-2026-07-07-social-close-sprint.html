Post 1 · Payment

支付不是小事，是路線舒適度

The first stress in regional China is often not the scenery.

It is payment, apps, hotel addresses, taxis and late transfers.

For foreign travellers, WeChat Pay / Alipay setup is useful, but I would still plan in layers:
- mobile payment where available
- backup overseas card
- small RMB cash layer
- SMS that actually works
- hotel names and addresses saved in Chinese

This matters more if your trip goes beyond Beijing and Shanghai into Yunnan, Zhangjiajie, Dunhuang, Xinjiang, Sanya or Northeast China.

Post 2 · Senior / family

真正高級，是不讓家人太累

A beautiful China route can still be a bad trip if it exhausts the people travelling with you.

For parents, seniors or children, I would choose the route by comfort before attractions:
- fewer hotel changes
- easier transfers
- hotel location before hotel logo
- language support where friction is likely
- one quiet morning after a long travel day

Yunnan, Zhangjiajie, Dunhuang, Xinjiang, Sanya and Northeast China can all be wonderful. But each needs a different rhythm.

Post 3 · Beyond mega cities

北京上海之外，先選對節奏

After Beijing and Shanghai, China becomes more interesting and less automatic.

Yunnan is slower mornings and old towns.
Zhangjiajie is dramatic, but pacing matters.
Dunhuang is desert, caves and silence.
Xinjiang is huge and needs route control.
Northeast China is winter light and trains.
Sanya is soft landing and sea air.

The question is not "which place is most famous?"
The question is "which route can feel beautiful without becoming tiring?"
