- Public China travel communities already contain itinerary posts and quick-question threads, such as `r/travelchina` and `r/chinatravel`.
- Travel media keeps framing Yunnan as a deeper, culture-rich route beyond first-time China city tourism. CN Traveler describes Yunnan through the Tea Horse Road, Lijiang, Dali-area infrastructure and lodge-based travel; El Pais highlights Dali, Shaxi, Lijiang and Shangri-La as a distinct Yunnan route.
- China travel planning still has friction for foreigners: payments, apps, train booking, language and local logistics. That is exactly the problem our route note solves.

English reply:

If this is your first Yunnan route, I would not try to change cities every day. A comfortable shape is usually Dali for lake/old-town pacing, Shaxi for one slower night, then Lijiang or Baisha for the snow-mountain side. The hard part is not the number of sights; it is hotel location, car time, language/app friction and how tired the group gets.

## Qualification Script

When someone replies "interested", ask:

1. 大概哪個月份出發？
2. 幾位同行？有小孩或長輩嗎？
3. 想走一週，還是一週多一點？
4. 預算比較接近 RMB 8,800/人起，還是更重視住宿舒適？
5. 用 email、WhatsApp、LINE 還是微信聯繫比較方便？

If they answer at least 3 of these, count as a warm lead. If they leave contact details through `/quick/`, count as a form submission.
