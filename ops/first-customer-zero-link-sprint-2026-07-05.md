# First Customer Zero-Link Sprint — 2026-07-05

Purpose: get replies before dropping links. Use these when platform rules are strict, when an account is new, or when a visible link would make the brand look like an ad.

Success is not posting volume. Success is one person replying with month, group size, days, comfort/budget and a contact method.

## Operating Rule

1. First public touch: no link, answer the actual question.
2. Ask one useful qualifying question at the end.
3. If the person replies with at least three planning fields, move them to direct conversation.
4. Only then send the matching tracked form or page.

## No-Link Replies

### Yunnan Route Too Rushed

```text
For Yunnan, I would plan rhythm before attractions. Dali, Shaxi and Lijiang/Baisha each need a different kind of time. The common mistake is changing cities every day and then losing the quiet parts of the trip.

If you have about a week, I would usually protect one slower Dali day, one Shaxi night, and then use Lijiang or Baisha for the snow-mountain side. The decision depends less on famous sights and more on hotel location, car time, luggage, and how tired the group gets.

If you share your travel month, number of travellers and total days, I can suggest whether this route is too tight.
```

### Second Trip To China

```text
If you have already done Beijing or Shanghai, I would choose the next China route by friction level. Yunnan is good when you want old towns, snow-mountain distance and a slower atmosphere. Zhangjiajie is dramatic but needs careful pacing. Dunhuang is beautiful if you want desert/Silk Road feeling. Xinjiang is larger and needs more route control.

For a second China trip, the right question is not only "what is beautiful?" It is "what can be comfortable with my dates, transfer tolerance and hotel expectations?"

What month are you going, and how many days do you have outside the big cities?
```

### China Payment / WeChat Pay

```text
I would plan China payments in layers, not as one perfect method.

Set up Alipay and/or WeChat Pay where available, keep a second card, carry a small RMB cash layer, and make sure the phone number tied to the cards can receive SMS verification. Some overseas cards can work for supported consumption scenarios, but setup, issuer and merchant acceptance can still vary.

If the trip goes beyond Beijing and Shanghai, payment readiness also affects route comfort: taxi pickup, hotel communication, tickets, late arrivals and transfer days.

Which cities or regions are you visiting? The practical risk is different for Beijing/Shanghai versus Yunnan, Xinjiang, Dunhuang or smaller towns.
```

### Zhangjiajie Senior Route

```text
For senior travellers, I would choose the Zhangjiajie route by comfort and risk, not by the most dramatic view.

The key checks are knees, fear of heights, balance, queue tolerance and whether a long walk after the cable car will affect the rest of the day. If either traveller is cautious with stairs or vertigo, choose the simpler cable-car/escalator-heavy shape and avoid combining too many scenic zones in one day.

What month are they going, where are they staying, and can they handle stairs comfortably?
```

### Guangzhou Luxury Family Hotel

```text
With a child, I would choose Guangzhou hotel area by daily rhythm before brand.

Tianhe luxury hotels can be strong for polished service, high-rise views and business-district convenience. White Swan / Shamian can feel gentler and more rooted in the city. A value hotel can work, but only if breakfast, taxi pickup, lobby comfort and evening surroundings still feel easy with a child.

Is Guangzhou a short city stop, or part of a wider China route with a train or airport transfer next?
```

### Warm Network Referral

```text
I am helping foreign travellers plan deeper China trips beyond Beijing and Shanghai. The first useful filter is not a package; it is checking whether the route is actually comfortable: payment, phone/SMS, hotel area, transfer time, language handoff and daily pace.

If you know someone considering China this year or next year, especially Yunnan, Zhangjiajie, Dunhuang, Xinjiang, Sanya or Northeast China, please introduce them. I can first reply with a route note and starting quote direction.
```
