# Bluehour China Lead Follow-Up Playbook

This is an internal guide for responding after a traveller submits the website form.

## First Reply Goal

The first reply should not feel like a package quote pasted from an agency. It should feel like a short private note from someone who has read the traveller's brief.

Every first reply should include:

- A short acknowledgement of the landscape they want
- A travel-journal style route note
- Why the route fits or where it may be too rushed
- A starting quote or starting range
- What is included and what is not included
- Three to five follow-up questions before a formal proposal

## Suggested Structure

Subject:
Bluehour China route note for your trip

Opening:
Thank you for sharing your China travel brief. I read the season, group size, comfort level and destination you are considering. Below is a first route note, not a fixed package yet.

Route note:
Write 2-4 short paragraphs that help the traveller imagine the trip. Mention morning, road pace, lodging atmosphere, food, language support, weather or distance only when relevant.

Starting quote:
Use the public starting prices as a floor, then explain what changes the price: season, hotel level, room setup, vehicle, guide language and local-provider availability.

Next questions:
Ask only what is needed to refine the route. Keep it calm and practical.

## First-Customer Qualification Priority

Treat these source angles as high priority because they already show a planning decision, not casual browsing:

- `natural_wonders_15d`: traveller is choosing one nature stop inside a fixed China route.
- `zhangjiajie_senior_route`: traveller has mobility, stairs, heights or route-risk constraints.
- `guangzhou_luxury_family`: traveller is comparing hotel comfort, service level and family logistics.
- `payment_setup`: traveller is preparing for China, but may not yet have a route decision.

Reply order:

1. If contact method plus 3 route fields are present, send a route note and starting quote within 12 hours.
2. If route fields are present but contact is weak, ask for the best email or WhatsApp first.
3. If only a narrow practical question is present, answer it, then ask one route-qualifying question.

## English Template: Natural Wonder In 15 Days

Subject:
Bluehour China route note for your 15-day China trip

```text
Thank you for sharing the route context. With Shanghai, Xi'an and Beijing already in the plan, I would choose the extra nature stop by friction, not fame.

My first read is this: if you want the cleanest add-on, Huangshan and Hongcun are the safest shape. If your priority is dramatic scenery and you accept more movement, Jiuzhaigou and Huanglong can be stronger. Yunnan becomes more attractive only if you can protect enough nights for Dali, Shaxi and Lijiang rather than treating it as a quick scenic stop.

Before giving a firmer route note, I would confirm:
1. Exact nights planned for Shanghai, Xi'an and Beijing
2. Whether one internal flight is acceptable
3. Comfort level for hotel moves and station transfers
4. Whether the priority is mountain drama, old-town atmosphere or easier logistics
5. Approximate budget per traveller for land arrangements

Once I have that, I can narrow the route to one clean skeleton and tell you where private transfer or local support would actually matter.
```

## English Template: Zhangjiajie Senior Route

Subject:
Bluehour China route note for Zhangjiajie comfort

```text
Thank you for the details. For senior travellers, I would design Zhangjiajie around comfort and risk before the most dramatic view.

The key variables are knees, balance, fear of heights, queue tolerance and how much walking should remain after the cable car or escalator sections. In many cases, the better route is the one that keeps one strong mountain experience and protects the evening, rather than combining too many zones in one day.

To narrow this safely, I would confirm:
1. Ages and any knee, balance or vertigo concerns
2. Hotel area and arrival/departure timing
3. Whether glass walks are desired or optional
4. How long a walking day feels comfortable
5. Whether private transfer support is preferred for awkward legs

Then I can reply with a calmer route shape and a starting quote for the land arrangements if local support is needed.
```

## English Template: Guangzhou Luxury Family Hotel

Subject:
Bluehour China hotel and route note for Guangzhou

```text
Thank you for the family context. For Guangzhou with a young child, I would choose the daily rhythm before the hotel brand.

A polished Tianhe luxury stay can work well if skyline, service and taxi movement are the priority. A river or Shamian-area choice can feel gentler and more rooted in Guangzhou. A value hotel can still be right, but only if breakfast, lobby comfort, taxi access and evening surroundings do not add friction with a child.

To make a useful recommendation, I would confirm:
1. Exact month and number of nights in Guangzhou
2. Whether Guangzhou is the whole trip or part of a wider China route
3. Child age and room setup needs
4. Hotel budget per night or comfort level
5. Whether airport/train transfers or private local support matter

With those details, I can suggest the hotel area first, then the hotel level, and connect it to the next transfer day.
```
