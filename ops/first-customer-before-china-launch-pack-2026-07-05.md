## Positioning

Do not sell this as a generic travel tip. Sell it as a calm pre-trip filter:

- If the traveller is only doing Beijing and Shanghai, basic prep may be enough.
- If they are going to Yunnan, Xinjiang, Dunhuang, Sanya or Northeast China, payment, data, language, hotels and transport timing become route-quality issues.
- The consultation offer is: leave the route, season, group size and comfort level; we reply with a practical route note and starting quote.

## Reply When Someone Says "Can I Just Use My Visa Card?"

```text
I would not plan China around card acceptance alone. Prepare mobile payment where available, bring a backup card, keep a little cash, and make sure your phone can receive SMS verification. Some overseas cards can be linked for supported WeChat Pay consumption scenarios, but availability still depends on setup, issuer and merchant.

If you are also planning regional travel, check the route pace at the same time. Payment friction plus long transfers can make a beautiful route feel harder than it needs to be.
```

## Reply When Someone Asks "Is China Hard For Foreign Tourists?"

```text
China is very manageable if the quiet practical layer is handled before landing: payment, data, SMS verification, hotel addresses, translation and route timing. It becomes harder when travellers leave the first cities without checking these basics.

For Beijing/Shanghai, infrastructure absorbs many mistakes. For Yunnan, Xinjiang, Dunhuang or Northeast China, I would check both the route and practical setup before booking everything.
```
